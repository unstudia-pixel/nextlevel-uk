Got Questions

Sword FAQs

Are replica swords legal to own in the UK?
Yes. Replica and decorative swords are entirely legal to purchase and own in the UK as an adult. Our products are sold as collectibles and display pieces. They cannot be carried in public. If you have specific questions about a product's classification, our team is happy to help before you buy.
What's the difference between Foam, Wooden and Metal swords?
Foam LARP swords are lightweight, safe for events, and great for beginners. Wooden cosplay swords are heavier, display-ready with authentic detail. Metal collector swords are full stainless steel replicas with wall plaques and premium sheaths - the choice for serious collectors. Our Blade Guide above breaks this down in detail.
Do your metal swords come sharp?
All our metal swords are sold as decorative replicas and are blunt-edged for safety. They are not functional weapons. The finish and weight replicate the originals faithfully, but they are display and collector pieces only.
Do swords come with sheaths or display stands?
Most of our wooden and metal swords include a sheath and/or display stand. Premium metal replicas often include a wall plaque for mounting. Each product listing clearly states what's included - check the product description before ordering. We never sell incomplete sets.
How quickly will my sword be dispatched?
All in-stock orders are dispatched within 48 hours. UK orders typically arrive in 2-3 working days. European and international orders take 5-10 working days depending on destination. You'll receive full tracking information the moment your order ships.
What sword would you recommend for a first-time buyer?
Start with a wooden cosplay sword in the £25-£45 range - great quality, authentic detail, without the premium price of metal. The Zoro Shusui, Tanjiro Fire Breath, and Sting Deluxe are our most popular first purchases. If you want to go straight to metal, the Zangetsu Katana at £63.99 is our best-value Legendary Grade pick.
